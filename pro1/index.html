Introduction of VR/AR

With groundbreaking technology development over the recent years, virtual reality and augmented reality have gained substantial social attention and acquired tremendous capital investment. However, the emerging technologies themselves still face many development obstacles such as social acceptance, hardware and software performance limitations.

Looking through the history of personal computers and mobile phones, we can’t help but noticing a similar trend -- it seems that technologies go through ups and downs before it matures and becomes part of the general public’s daily life. Based on these historical datasets of personal computers and mobile phones, can we apply the same assumption and make a similar prediction on the future of VR/AR technology in the years to come? According to Moore’s law, the number of transistors in a dense integrated circuit doubles approximately every two years. Are we at the dawn of a new wave of immersive technologies?
